🚨 Scam of the Week

The Medicare Callback Scam

The call comes in showing a real Medicare number. The caller says your parent's benefits will be suspended unless they call back immediately. When they call, they're told to verify their identity — with a gift card. Median loss: $1,400. Over 40,000 reports filed last quarter alone.

⚡ Quick Alerts
IRS Suspension Scam — "Your Social Security number has been suspended due to suspicious activity. Press 1 to speak with an agent."
Grandparent Voice-Clone Scam — AI now fakes your kid's voice. "Grandma, I'm in jail. Please don't tell Mom. I need $2,000 in gift cards."
💬 What to tell your parents

"If anyone calls from Medicare and asks you to call back — don't. Hang up and call me first. Medicare never calls asking for gift cards, ever."
