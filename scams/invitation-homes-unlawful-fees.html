Unmasking the Invitation Homes Unlawful Fees and Charges Scam

Published 2026-03-15 · Is This a Scam? · isthisascam.news

The Federal Trade Commission (FTC) is refunding more than $47.2 million to consumers who were misled into paying for undisclosed fees and illegal charges by Invitation Homes, a major landlord of single-family homes in America. This scam highlights the vulnerability of older adults who may have been duped by complex fee structures or lack of clear communication.

How It Works

The scam begins when renters sign a lease agreement with Invitation Homes without realizing they are being charged for hidden fees and undisclosed costs. These charges could include application fees, background check fees, and other expenses that were not clearly explained upfront. The FTC found that these fees were often buried in fine print or communicated in a way that was confusing or misleading to renters.

Why It Works on Older Adults

Older adults are particularly vulnerable to this type of scam due to several factors, including a tendency to trust authority figures and less familiarity with the intricacies of rental agreements. Additionally, many older adults may not have the technological savvy or access to legal resources needed to spot these hidden fees.

Real Numbers

The FTC has refunded over $47.2 million to consumers affected by this scam. Thousands of older adults were duped into paying unlawful charges between January 2021 and September 2024, highlighting the widespread impact of this deceptive practice.

Red Flags to Watch For

  • Any request for personal information over the phone or email should be treated with caution.
  • A message claiming you’ve won a prize but requires additional payment is likely fraudulent.
  • Unusual fees or charges that were not clearly explained during the signing of the lease agreement.
  • Pop-ups on your computer screen warning of security issues and demanding immediate remote access.

What to Do Right Now

To protect yourself or an older adult, take these steps:

  1. Contact Invitation Homes directly if you suspect you were charged for undisclosed fees. Verify all charges through your lease agreement and documentation.
  2. Check the FTC’s website for updates on refunds and eligibility criteria.
  3. Talk to a parent or loved one about this scam using this script: 'Mom/Dad, have you received any unusual fee notices from Invitation Homes? We need to verify these together to ensure we’re not being scammed.'

Report It

If you suspect fraud, report it immediately to the Federal Trade Commission at 1-877-382-4357 or visit their website for more information. You can also file a complaint with the FTC online.
