Government Grant Scam: How It Works and How to Protect Yourself

Published 2026-03-15 · Is This a Scam? · isthisascam.news

Victims of the government grant scam have been conned out of hundreds of thousands of dollars, often by providing personal information under false pretenses. This scam preys on older adults with promises of easy money.

How It Works

The scam starts with a phone call or email claiming you’ve won a government grant worth $50,000. The caller might say, “Congratulations! The government has selected you to receive $50,000 in grants. Please provide your Social Security number and bank details.” They pressure the victim into providing personal information immediately, which is then used for identity theft or to withdraw funds directly from their accounts.

Why It Works

This scam exploits older adults’ trust in government institutions and a desire for financial assistance. The promise of free money is particularly appealing during economic hardships. Scammers use urgency and the authority of government agencies to create fear, making victims act quickly without questioning the authenticity.

Red Flags

  • Unexpected notification about winning money from an unknown source.
  • Requests for personal information like Social Security numbers or bank details over the phone or email.
  • Urgency to provide information immediately, often with threats of missing out on the grant.

What to Do

Hang up immediately if you receive such a call. If your parent is hesitant:

“Mom/Dad, I know it sounds exciting, but this is likely a scam. The government does not randomly award grants over the phone or ask for personal information like that.”

Contact the Federal Trade Commission (FTC) at 1-877-FTC-HELP (382-4357) to report any suspicious activity.
