Government Grant Scam Alert: Protecting Older Adults from Financial Fraud

Published 2026-03-15 · Is This a Scam? · isthisascam.news

A recent scam has seen millions lost when fraudsters pretend to offer free government grants. Victims are often told they’ve won thousands, only to be asked for personal information or upfront fees.

How It Works

The scam typically begins with a phone call or email. The caller claims to represent a legitimate government agency and states that you’ve been selected to receive a grant. They might say something like: 'Congratulations! We’ve found additional funds for your expenses.'

To claim the money, they ask for personal information such as Social Security numbers, bank account details, or even request an upfront fee to process the grant. Once they have this data, they can commit identity theft and drain financial accounts.

Why It Works

Older adults are often targeted because they may be more trusting of official-sounding calls and emails. The promise of free money is particularly alluring, especially if the recipient is on a fixed income or facing unexpected expenses.

The fear of missing out on legitimate government benefits can also cloud judgment, making it harder to spot the red flags.

Red Flags

  • Requests for personal information, including Social Security numbers and bank account details.
  • Demands for payment upfront to process or claim the grant.
  • Pressure to act quickly without verifying through official channels.

What to Do

If you receive a call or email about a government grant, hang up immediately and do not respond. Contact the appropriate agency directly using verified contact information from their official website to confirm if there is any legitimate opportunity.

To talk to your parent: 'Mom/Dad, I heard about this scam where they pretend to offer free money through grants. If you get a call or email like that, don't respond. We can check it out together on the official government website.'
