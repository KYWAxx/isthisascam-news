Protecting Older Adults from the Boss Impersonation Gift Card Scam

Published 2026-03-15 · Is This a Scam? · isthisascam.news

Older adults are being targeted with a sophisticated scam where fraudsters impersonate their bosses, demanding immediate purchase of expensive gift cards. In just one reported case, an older adult lost over $5,000 to this deceptive scheme.

How It Works

The scam begins with a call or email that appears to be from your boss. The fraudster might say something like: 'Can you quickly purchase these gift cards for our company event? We need them right away.' Often, the request is sent directly via text message to create a sense of urgency.

Why It Works

Older adults are particularly vulnerable because they often have a strong desire to please authority figures and avoid conflict. The sudden demand for action can trigger anxiety and fear, leading them to act without double-checking the request's authenticity. Additionally, older adults may be less familiar with common scam tactics, making them more susceptible.

Red Flags

  • Unexpected requests for gift card purchases via text or email.
  • A sense of urgency in completing the task immediately.
  • Lack of personal interaction to verify the request's legitimacy.

What to Do

1. Always ask your parent to check with their boss directly before purchasing any gift cards, even if it seems urgent.

2. Use this script: 'Before I purchase these gift cards, can we confirm this in person?'

  • Talk to your parents about the importance of verifying requests in person or via a known phone number, not through text messages or emails.
