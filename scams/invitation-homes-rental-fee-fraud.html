Invitation Homes Rental Fee Fraud: Unveiling Hidden Fees That Cost Over $47.2 Million in Refunds

Published 2026-03-15 · Is This a Scam? · isthisascam.news

The Federal Trade Commission (FTC) has issued checks totaling more than $47.2 million to consumers deceived by Invitation Homes, a large landlord of single-family homes. This refund is due to undisclosed fees and unlawful charges that affected renters between January 2021 and September 2024. Older adults were particularly vulnerable as they often rely on stable rental agreements without hidden costs.

How It Works

The scam began with a deceptive contract that included hidden fees not disclosed to the tenants. Invitation Homes buried these additional charges within the paperwork, leading victims to unknowingly overpay for extended periods. For example, renters might have been charged extra for maintenance or property management without clear disclosure.

Why It Works on Older Adults

Older adults are often targeted because they tend to prioritize trust and stability in their living arrangements. Scammers exploit this by presenting themselves as a reputable company like Invitation Homes, leading victims to overlook the fine print or believe that all terms were agreed upon during signing.

Real Numbers

The FTC has refunded over $47.2 million to affected consumers. This scam impacted thousands of renters who were unknowingly subjected to hidden fees and unlawful charges for years. The scope of this fraud highlights the significant financial burden placed on older adults, many of whom may be living on fixed incomes.

Red Flags to Watch For

  • 'Congratulations! We’ve found additional funds for your expenses.' (Government Grant Scam)
  • 'We are from Microsoft tech support. Your PC is infected, we can fix it.' (Tech Support Scam)
  • 'I need these gift cards urgently for business purposes.' (Gift Card Scam)

What to Do Right Now

To protect your parent:

  1. Contact Invitation Homes directly and request a review of their rental agreement.
  2. Check the FTC's website to see if they are eligible for a refund. Visit ftc.gov.
  3. Talk to your parent using this script: 'Mom/Dad, have you received any additional fees from Invitation Homes that were not disclosed in the initial contract? It's important we check if you're entitled to a refund.'

Report It

If you suspect hidden fees or fraudulent charges, report it immediately. Contact the FTC at 1-877-FTC-HELP (1-877-382-4357) or file a complaint online at ftc.gov.
