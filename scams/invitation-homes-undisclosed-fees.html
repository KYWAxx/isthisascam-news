How Invitation Homes Charged Hidden Fees and What Older Adults Need to Know

Published 2026-03-15 · Is This a Scam? · isthisascam.news

Invitation Homes, one of the largest landlords of single-family homes in the U.S., has been accused by the Federal Trade Commission (FTC) for charging undisclosed fees that have cost renters millions. More than $47.2 million is being refunded to eligible consumers who paid these hidden charges between January 2021 and September 2024.

How It Works

The scam operates by charging renters additional fees without proper disclosure or justification. For example, a renter might receive a bill that includes an extra charge for 'property management services' or 'maintenance costs.' When questioned, the staff at Invitation Homes would insist these charges were standard and legal, even though they were often not.

Why It Works on Older Adults

Older adults are particularly vulnerable to this scam due to their reliance on financial stability from rental agreements. Many older adults live on fixed incomes and may be less likely to question additional charges out of fear or a lack of knowledge about their rights as tenants.

Real Numbers

The FTC is sending checks totaling more than $47.2 million to eligible consumers who paid these hidden charges between January 2021 and September 2024. According to the FBI’s Internet Crime Complaint Center (IC3), $3.4 billion was stolen from adults over 60 last year, highlighting the significant impact of such scams on older populations.

Red Flags to Watch For

  • You receive a bill with additional undisclosed fees that are not explained or justified.
  • Rental staff insists these charges are standard without providing clear documentation or legal justification.
  • The scam mirrors other fraudulent schemes, such as the Amazon Prime Settlement Scam where you might see phrases like 'You are eligible for a refund. Click here to claim it.'
  • Gift Card Scams may involve urgent requests like 'Can you purchase these gift cards immediately?'
  • Government Grant Scams often ask for personal information or bank details with promises of free money.

What to Do Right Now

1. Review your rental agreements and bills for any undisclosed fees. If you notice charges that were not previously disclosed, contact the FTC at 1-877-FTC-HELP (382-4357).

2. Verify any refund or settlement offers through official channels such as the FTC website.

3. Talk to your parents about these scams using a script like this: 'Mom/Dad, I read about a scam where landlords are charging hidden fees. Can we go over your recent bills together? If you see any charges that weren't disclosed before, let's report them.'

Report It

If you suspect fraud or have been a victim of this scheme, contact the FTC at 1-877-FTC-HELP (382-4357) or file a complaint online at ftc.gov. Additionally, report any fraudulent activity to your local law enforcement and the FBI IC3 at 1-800-CALL-FBI (225-5324).
