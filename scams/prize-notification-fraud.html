Unveiling the Prize Notification Fraud: A Billion-Dollar Threat to Older Adults

Published 2026-03-15 · Is This a Scam? · isthisascam.news

Scammers are preying on dreams of winning big with a fraudulent scheme that steals millions from unsuspecting victims. The most alarming detail? Many older adults lose their life savings after being told they've won $10,000 in a holiday giveaway, only to find out it was all a lie.

How It Works

The scam begins with an unexpected phone call or text message. Scammers often spoof the caller ID to display a legitimate-sounding company's name like 'ABC Sweepstakes.' The script is meticulously designed for urgency: “Congratulations! You’ve won $10,000 in our holiday giveaway!” The caller then provides instructions on how to claim the prize, which typically involves sending money via gift cards, wire transfers, or prepaid debit cards to cover taxes and shipping fees. This convinces victims they’re making an obligation to claim a prize when it’s just another way for fraudsters to steal their hard-earned savings.

Why It Works on Older Adults

Psychologically, older adults may be more susceptible due to the emotional appeal of winning something big and the fear of missing out. Additionally, many older adults are less familiar with common scam tactics or may have fewer people around them to verify the legitimacy of such calls. The urgency and the promise of a large sum of money can overwhelm their critical thinking.

Real Numbers

Last year alone, over $30 million was lost by individuals falling victim to prize notification scams, according to FBI IC3 data. These numbers underscore the severity and prevalence of this scam, which has affected thousands of older adults nationwide.

Red Flags to Watch For

  • The message says “You’ve won $10,000 in our holiday giveaway!” Scammers use urgency and big numbers to create a sense of excitement.
  • The caller asks for bank details or other personal information to process the prize. This is always a red flag for scams needing immediate action.
  • Instructions to buy gift cards quickly to claim the prize. This is how scammers get their money without revealing their identity.
  • Requests to send money via wire transfer or prepaid debit card to cover taxes and shipping fees.

What to Do Right Now

1. Verify any prize notification through official company channels, not the number provided in the message.
2. Speak with your parent about recent calls or texts they’ve received using this script: “Mom/Dad, have you gotten a call or text saying you won $10,000? These are scams. Let’s talk more about it.”
3. Report any suspicious activity to the FTC at 1-877-FTC-HELP (1-877-382-4357).

Report It

If you or someone you know is a victim of prize notification fraud, report it immediately to the Federal Trade Commission (FTC) at 1-877-FTC-HELP (1-877-382-4357). You can also file a complaint online at ftccomplaintassistant.gov. Reporting scams helps protect others from falling victim.
