Invitation Homes Rental Fee Deception: Protecting Older Adults from Hidden Costs

Published 2026-03-15 · Is This a Scam? · isthisascam.news

Older adults have been deceived out of millions in hidden fees by Invitation Homes, one of the largest landlords for single-family homes in the U.S. The company has refunded over $47.2 million to victims who paid undisclosed charges between January 2021 and September 2024.

How It Works

The scam starts when Invitation Homes tenants are told they must pay additional fees for services or repairs that were not clearly communicated in their lease agreements. These hidden costs include maintenance, cleaning, and management fees that were not disclosed at the time of signing. For example, a tenant might receive an unexpected bill for $300 after reporting a broken appliance, only to find out later that this fee was never mentioned in writing.

Why It Works on Older Adults

Older adults are particularly vulnerable due to their reliance on fixed incomes and less familiarity with modern rental practices. Scammers exploit the trust older tenants place in established companies like Invitation Homes, making it easier for hidden fees to go unnoticed until significant sums have been paid.

Real Numbers

The FTC has refunded over $47.2 million to eligible consumers who were deceived by Invitation Homes’ unlawful charges. The scam affected thousands of older adults living in single-family homes across the U.S., many of whom did not realize they had been overcharged until it was too late.

Red Flags to Watch For

  • Sudden unexpected bills for services or repairs that were not clearly disclosed at the time of signing the lease agreement.
  • Tenants being asked to pay fees for services that are typically included in rental costs, such as maintenance and cleaning.
  • Invoices with vague descriptions or unclear charges that seem out of place compared to previous bills.
  • Pressure from landlords to make payments quickly without providing detailed explanations.

What to Do Right Now

If you suspect your aging parent has been a victim of hidden rental fees, take the following steps:

  • Contact Invitation Homes directly and request an itemized list of all charges for the past year. Compare these with the original lease agreement.
  • Reach out to the FTC at 1-877-382-4357 or file a complaint online if you find discrepancies.
  • Talk to your parent using this script: 'Mom/Dad, I've heard about hidden fees from Invitation Homes. Can we go over our bills together?'

Report It

If you suspect that you or someone you know has been a victim of the Invitation Homes scam, report it to the Federal Trade Commission at 1-877-382-4357 or visit FTC Complaint Assistant. Additionally, file a complaint with your state’s Attorney General office.
