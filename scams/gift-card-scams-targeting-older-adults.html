Gift Card Scams Targeting Older Adults: Over $10 Million Stolen

Published 2026-03-15 · Is This a Scam? · isthisascam.news

Scammers posing as bosses have stolen over $10 million by tricking victims into buying gift cards under false pretenses. This scam often targets older adults, exploiting their trust and fear of job loss.

How It Works

The scam starts with a call or text message pretending to be from your boss. The script is chillingly simple: 'I need these for an urgent business expense, please send me the card numbers now.' Scammers rely on urgency and authority to get victims to act quickly without verifying.

Why It Works

This scam exploits a mix of fear, trust in authority figures, and often limited digital literacy. Older adults may be more likely to believe their boss is asking for help, especially if they are concerned about job security or feel pressured by the urgency conveyed over the phone or text.

Red Flags

  • Sudden requests for gift cards instead of traditional payment methods.
  • Demands for immediate action without allowing time to verify with a supervisor in person or through another channel.
  • Contact via unfamiliar phone numbers or unsecured communication platforms like text messages.

What to Do

To protect yourself and your loved ones:

  • Always verify any urgent request for gift cards by contacting the actual supervisor using a known, verified contact method. Don't use the number provided in the message.
  • Talk to your parents or older relatives about this scam: 'Mom/Dad, if someone says they're from work asking you to buy gift cards, can we check with [supervisor's name] first?'
