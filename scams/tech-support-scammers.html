Tech Support Scams: The Alarming Details You Need to Know

Published 2026-03-15 · Is This a Scam? · isthisascam.news

A pop-up message claiming your computer is infected with malware can lead to a scammer demanding hundreds of dollars for bogus fixes.

How It Works

The scam starts with an alarming pop-up on your screen: “Your PC has been infected with malware.” The message instructs you to call the number displayed immediately. When you do, someone claiming to be from Microsoft tech support answers and says they can fix the issue for a fee.

Here’s exactly what they say:

“We are from Microsoft tech support. Your PC is infected with malware, which can steal your data or crash your computer. We can help you fix this issue.”

Why It Works

The combination of a sudden threat and the promise of immediate assistance makes these scams particularly effective. Older adults often feel less confident about their tech skills and are more likely to trust an authoritative voice offering help. The fear of losing precious data or having their computer crash creates urgency, making it easier for scammers to exploit.

Red Flags

  • A pop-up claiming your computer is infected with malware, especially if it demands a call or payment immediately.
  • Suddenly being contacted by someone who claims to be from Microsoft tech support over the phone about an issue you didn’t report.
  • Requests for remote access to your computer or for credit card information to pay for “repairs.”

What to Do

If you suspect a scam, take these steps:

  1. Close the pop-up immediately and do not call the number provided.
  2. Contact trusted technical support from your service provider or a local IT professional. They can scan and clean your computer safely.

To talk to an older adult about this, use this script:

“Hey Mom/Dad, have you seen any pop-ups on the computer claiming it’s infected? Those are scams. If you see one, just close it and call me right away.”
