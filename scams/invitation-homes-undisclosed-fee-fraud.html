Invitation Homes Undisclosed Fee Fraud: How Older Adults Are Losing Money to Deceptive Practices

Published 2026-03-15 · Is This a Scam? · isthisascam.news

The Federal Trade Commission (FTC) has sent out checks totaling more than $47.2 million to consumers who were charged undisclosed and unlawful fees by Invitation Homes, one of the largest landlords in the U.S. This fraudulent practice specifically targets older adults who are often on fixed incomes and rely heavily on financial stability from their rental agreements.

How It Works

The scam unfolds when tenants receive notices or calls about additional fees that were not disclosed at the time of signing their lease agreements. These fees can range from administrative charges to maintenance costs, often adding up significantly over time. The script used by Invitation Homes typically goes like this: 'We are required to collect a small fee for certain services provided to ensure your home remains in top condition. Please make sure to include this amount with your next rent payment.'

Why It Works on Older Adults

Older adults often have limited financial resources and may be less likely to question additional charges from a landlord they trust or believe is legally entitled to these fees. The combination of fixed incomes, reliance on stable housing arrangements, and sometimes diminished cognitive abilities makes them particularly vulnerable to deceptive practices.

Real Numbers

The FTC has sent out more than $47.2 million in refunds to eligible consumers who paid hidden charges between January 2021 and September 2024. According to the FBI's Internet Crime Complaint Center (IC3), older adults lost a staggering $3.4 billion to scams last year alone.

Red Flags to Watch For

  • You receive notices or calls about additional fees that were not disclosed in your lease agreement.
  • The landlord insists on immediate payment for unspecified services without providing detailed information.
  • You are asked to pay for charges that seem excessive or unrelated to your living conditions.

What to Do Right Now

1. Check if you have received any undisclosed fees in the past and report them to the FTC at www.ftc.gov.

2. Review your lease agreement for any hidden clauses that might allow additional charges.

3. Use this script when talking to a parent: 'Mom/Dad, have you ever been asked to pay extra fees by Invitation Homes? The FTC is sending refunds to people who paid these hidden costs.'

Report It

If you suspect fraudulent activity or undisclosed charges from your landlord, report it immediately to the Federal Trade Commission at 1-877-FTC-HELP (1-877-382-4357) or visit www.ftc.gov. Additionally, file a complaint with your local consumer protection agency.
